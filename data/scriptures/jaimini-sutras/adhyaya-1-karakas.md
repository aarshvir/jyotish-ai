## Chara Karaka System

**JAIMINI 1.1** — The seven Chara Karakas (variable significators) are assigned based on the longitude of each planet in the birth chart, from highest to lowest degree: Atmakaraka (AK), Amatyakaraka (AmK), Bhratrukaraka (BK), Matrukaraka (MK), Putrakaraka (PK), Gnatikaraka (GK), Darakaraka (DK). Rahu follows special rules and is typically excluded from the Chara Karaka scheme, or counted as the 8th karaka.

**JAIMINI 1.2** — The Atmakaraka (AK) is the planet with the highest degree (ignoring sign) in the natal chart. It represents the soul's highest purpose — the primary karmic lesson the native must master in this lifetime. The Atmakaraka's natal placement, sign dignity, and dasha timing reveal the central arc of the life story.

**JAIMINI 1.3** — The Darakaraka (DK) is the planet with the lowest degree in the natal chart. It governs the spouse and marriage partner. The sign containing the DK in D-9 (Navamsha), and the planets aspecting or joining the DK, describe the character and quality of the spouse.

## Jaimini Aspects

**JAIMINI 1.4** — Jaimini aspects (Rashi Dristi) are sign-based, not degree-based. Fixed signs aspect all four cardinals. Cardinal signs aspect all four fixed. Dual signs aspect each other. This creates a different aspect grid from Parashari aspects, enabling unique timing calculations.

**JAIMINI 1.5** — Fixed signs (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius) aspect all four cardinal signs (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn) but NOT each other or the dual signs. Cardinal signs (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn) aspect all four fixed signs but NOT each other or the dual signs. Dual signs (Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces) aspect each other only.

## Karakamsha Lagna

**JAIMINI 1.6** — The Karakamsha Lagna is found by identifying the sign in D-9 (Navamsha) where the Atmakaraka falls. This becomes a special ascendant that reveals the soul's purpose, spiritual inclinations, and the field where the native will ultimately find fulfillment. Planets in Kendra from Karakamsha are particularly significant.

**JAIMINI 1.7** — Jupiter in Kendra from Karakamsha gives the native a life oriented toward dharmic service, teaching, and wisdom. They become known as a wise advisor. Venus in Kendra from Karakamsha gives luxurious tastes, artistic refinement, and pleasurable marriage. Saturn in Kendra from Karakamsha makes the native a disciplined worker who rises through consistent effort.

## Padapancha (Pada Lagna)

**JAIMINI 1.8** — Pada Lagna (Arudha Lagna, AL) is calculated as follows: count the number of signs the Lagna lord has moved from the Lagna, then count the same number of signs from the Lagna lord's position. The resulting sign is the Pada Lagna. This represents how the native is perceived by the world — their public image and reputation.

**JAIMINI 1.9** — Benefics in the 1st, 2nd, 4th, 5th, 7th, 8th, 10th, 11th from the Arudha Lagna promote the public image and social status of the native. Malefics in these positions can damage reputation or create public adversaries, depending on which houses they rule.

**JAIMINI 1.10** — The relationship between Lagna (soul) and Arudha Lagna (public image) reveals the alignment or disconnect between who the native truly is and how they are perceived. When both are in harmony (same sign, or in mutual Kendra/Trikona), the native's authentic self aligns with their public persona — creating trust and genuine influence.
