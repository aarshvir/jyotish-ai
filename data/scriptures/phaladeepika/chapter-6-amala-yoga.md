## Amala Yoga (Phaladeepika)

**PHAL 6.1** — Amala Yoga (Pure Fame Yoga) arises when the 10th house from the Lagna or the Moon is exclusively occupied by natural benefics (Jupiter, Venus, Mercury, and a beneficially-associated Moon). This yoga generates fame that is both widespread and enduring — the native's reputation survives long after their physical life.

**PHAL 6.2** — The specific quality of Amala Yoga is purity of means — the native achieves success without ethical compromise. Unlike some yogas that produce wealth through any means, Amala Yoga specifically indicates that the native's name is associated with noble deeds and righteous conduct.

**PHAL 6.3** — Jupiter in the 10th from Lagna gives the highest expression of Amala Yoga — the native becomes a spiritual teacher, wise counselor, or philanthropist whose deeds inspire generations. Venus in the 10th gives artistic fame and renown in creative fields. Mercury in the 10th gives scholarly distinction and commercial success built on genuine expertise.

## Sunapha, Anapha, and Durudhara Yogas

**PHAL 6.4** — Sunapha Yoga: When any planet other than the Sun occupies the 2nd house from the Moon (Moon's 2nd house), the native has self-earned wealth through personal capability and enterprise. The nature of the occupying planet determines the field of wealth.

**PHAL 6.5** — Anapha Yoga: When any planet other than the Sun occupies the 12th house from the Moon, the native has natural charisma, a dignified bearing, and is admired by all. The native achieves renown for their character and disposition.

**PHAL 6.6** — Durudhara Yoga: When planets occupy both the 2nd and 12th from the Moon (simultaneously creating both Sunapha and Anapha), Durudhara Yoga forms. The native enjoys the combined benefits — wealth and fame, enterprise and charisma, creating a well-rounded life of achievement.

## Shakata Yoga and Its Cancellation

**PHAL 6.7** — Shakata Yoga forms when Jupiter is in the 6th, 8th, or 12th from the Moon (not in a Kendra from the Moon). This yoga can cause the native to face setbacks, reversals of fortune, and difficulty maintaining steady progress. Gains may come only to be followed by losses.

**PHAL 6.8** — Shakata Yoga cancellation (Shakata Bhanga): When Jupiter in a harmful position from the Moon is simultaneously in a Kendra or Trikona from the Lagna, the Shakata Yoga is cancelled. The native still experiences some fluctuation, but the overall life trajectory remains positive due to Jupiter's Lagna-relative beneficence.

## Chandra-Lagna Combinations

**PHAL 6.9** — When the Moon and Lagna are the same sign (Moon in Lagna), the native has a strong, emotionally driven character — deeply identified with their emotional responses. The mind and body work in harmony, but the emotional patterns of early life are highly visible in the personality.

**PHAL 6.10** — When the Moon is in a Kendra from the Lagna, the native possesses innate mental stability and the emotional capacity to handle public life effectively. This is considered highly auspicious for public roles, journalism, politics, and any occupation requiring public trust.

**PHAL 6.11** — When the Moon and Lagna lord are in mutual Kendra or Trikona relationship, the native's emotions and identity work synergistically — creating a person whose inner life aligns with their outer expression. Such natives rarely experience the disconnect between public persona and private persona that plagues many.
